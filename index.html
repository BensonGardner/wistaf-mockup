How Wisconsin invests in civil justice for all.

Since 1986, the Wisconsin Trust Account Foundation (WisTAF) has aided in "the administration of justice," administering more than $61.8 million in civil legal aid grants to help people access justice.

Why did the Wisconsin Supreme Court establish WisTAF? Because the Constitution doesn't guarantee an attorney in civil cases. And yet, when people don't have attorneys, a hole opens in the ship of our legal system. Courts become less efficient. Clients' livelihood and safety are at stake. And when they can't access legal protections, taxpayers provide other supports, like police and emergency room services.

Justice shouldn't depend on how much money you make. Help us make this a reality.
