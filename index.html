How Wisconsin invests in civil justice for all.

Since 1986, the Wisconsin Trust Account Foundation (WisTAF) has aided in "the administration of justice" by overseeing funds and investing in services and projects, including over $61.8 million in grants, to increase people's access to justice in matters of civil law.

The Wisconsin Supreme Court established WisTAF because the Constitution doesn't guarantee an attorney in civil cases, yet we trust civil law to protect our safety and livelihood. WisTAF helps our system provide justice as intended — in turn benefitting our state as a whole.

Justice shouldn't depend on how much money you make. Help us make this a reality.
